# Communities of practice for user-centred design

The NHS App has a few types of community spaces: 

- **UCD meet-up.** Every other week there is a meet-up for all UCD people (and friends) who work on the App. The subject matter is typically teams presenting research and design work, mini-workshops, and updates on practice. 
- **Profession huddles.** These mostly happen every week and cover user research, service design, content design, and interaction design. They are a good place to bring work to get feedback or ask questions about how to approach a problem. These are open to non-App team members but attendance isn't expected. It is considered polite to message the relevant Slack channel ahead of a huddle if you have a novel challenge to address. 
- **UCD work-in-flight.** This is a fortnightly check-in for all of the teams working on NHS App things. It is essentially a scrum-of-scrums, for UCD. Attendance is mandatory for all of the teams that are officially within the NHS App programme and who have a substantial, live project that is being delivered via the App. The call is every other Monday, from 10:00 to 11:00.
