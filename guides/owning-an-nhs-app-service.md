# How to own an NHS App feature or service

The NHS App is composed of a multitude of features and services. Some of these are owned by the core app team, but many are owned by other teams across the NHS and some are provided by third-parties. It is one thing to _build_ a new service but it is an entirely other thing to _own_ it. Building a service is the process of designing, developing, and launching it. Owning a service is the ongoing work of maintaining it, improving it, and making sure it continues to meet user needs over time.

If you are going to build a new service for the NHS App, you should also be prepared to roll it out and then own it. This means that you will need to have a plan for how you will maintain and improve the service after it is launched. This includes things like:

- Making sure the wider system knows it exists and how to use it
- Monitoring user feedback and analytics to identify areas for improvement
- Keeping the service up to date with the latest design and development standards
- Responding to user needs and modifying the service as necessary to meet those needs
- Coordinating with other teams to ensure the service continues to fit well within the overall App experience

The core NHS App team can provide support and guidance on how to do all of these things, but ultimately it is up to the team that builds the service to own it. If you build something, you need to make sure you have plans for how to maintain it and improve it over time (including securing funding). This is a crucial part of building for the NHS App and is essential for ensuring that the App continues to meet user needs and provides a good experience for users.
